UN Deputy Humanitarian Chief Kyung-wha Kang visited Myanmar during the week that marked the two-year anniversary of the inter-communal violence that killed hundreds and displaced more than 140,000 people in Rakhine State. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Ms. Kang witnessed first-hand the enormous logistical challenges facing humanitarian workers in Rakhine. Many of the camps and isolated villages in need of assistance can only be reached by boat. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Ms. Kang described the conditions In Nget Chaung IDP camp as “appalling, with wholly inadequate access to basic services including health, education, water and sanitation.” Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Ms. Kang asked a woman why many young children in the camps were naked. The answer: “because they are too hot.” The corrugated roofs of the longhouses are good against the rain, but create oven-like temperatures during the heat of the day. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Camp committees described the reality of their lives as internally displaced people with severe restrictions to their freedom of movement outside of the camps. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
“I have four children and my husband is dead,” said Usnara Begom, 30 yrs old, who is unable to leave her neighbourhood. “If I can’t move freely, how can I find work to feed my family?” Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Of the estimated 25,000 children of primary-school age who live in IDP camps in Rakhine, about three quarters have had some access to emergency education provided by NGOs. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Despite the restrictions in freedom of movement, some goods manage to get through to shops in IDP camps, although prices vary widely and are often prohibitive. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Ms. Kang also visited host communities in Rakhine, which is one of the poorest States in Myanmar. Development needs are enormous and must be addressed by the Government and the international community in conjunction with the humanitarian, life-saving response in Rakhine. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
The thatch homes found in most villages across Rakhine State provide little protection from the storms and cyclones that regularly batter Myanmar’s coastline. The UN and NGOs are working closely with the Government in preparing for natural disasters. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
Over five days in Myanmar, Ms. Kang spent much of her time listening to stake-holders at all levels, including community leaders, humanitarian workers, as well as local and national authorities. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
The Myanmar Humanitarian Country Team estimates that $192 million will be needed in 2014 to assist 421,000 people with life-saving humanitarian aid in Myanmar. Credit: OCHA/P. Peron
