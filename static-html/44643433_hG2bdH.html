10 Sept 2014, Jamara, The Gambia: Hunger is worsening in The Gambia's Central River Region. A poor rainy season has left 27 per cent of people facing moderate to severe food insecurity - the worst levels in the country. Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
9 Sept 2014, Keshar Kunda, the Gambia: Issa is the Imam of the small village of Keshar Kunda. He is very worried about the situation: "It is a difficult year with late rains. Today I sold our last goat. I will have to ask my sister to help us out. Soon there will be nothing left." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Jamara, the Gambia: Filidie has started selling herbs to augment the small income she makes from working in the fields that surround her village of Jamara. "It's the only way to provide a meal for the children. But when my granddaughter fell sick, we had to use everything to buy medicine." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
11 Sept 2014, Sugga the Gambia: Fatou Sy lost her husband eight years ago. Her eldest son didn't find a job after finishing high school and left. "My daughters are married, so I work alone on my field. It's simply not enough. In the difficult months I have to rely on neighbours and family for help." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Jamara, the Gambia: Mohamed Bah cultivates his field with a plough and oxen lent from his neighbour. "We help each other, so that we have just enough to get along. But I can't pay proper medicine for my five-year old. He is sick since he was born." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Jamara, the Gambia: Danfa checks his crops. "The millet may be okay, but will need more time and rain to mature. To improve our harvest, we need better farming tools. That is the most important assistance." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Jamara, the Gambia: Father-of-seven Lamine Fall checks his groundnut crop. "This field should be covered by green plants by now. But the rains arrived several weeks later than expected, and the seeds were already in the ground. The harvest will be late, and bad." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Kudan Tenda, the Gambia: People across the Central River Region also rely on the river. The men often leave for several days to find the best fishing spots. Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Kudan Tenda, the Gambia, 10 Sep 2014: Ibrahima Sall and his nephew on their boat. "In good years, the river provides enough. But late and little rains also mean that the fish lay less eggs. So we are punished both in the fields, and on the river. This year, we most probably will need help." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
10 Sept 2014, Kudan Tenda, the Gambia: Aid groups are scaling up their support for families and communities affected by the worsening food situation. The World Food Programme, for example, plans to support more than 100,000 people with cash vouchers and food supplies. Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
