31 Oct 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: Fourteen year-old Ndeye picks niebe (beans) and groundnuts. “I take pride from working in the field and helping my father. But I also want to finish my education to become a police officer in the city.” During the harvest season, Ndeye heads to the fields every day after class. Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
31 Oct 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: Ndeye’s father, Beyhrar, farms with his three oldest daughters. "We rarely produce enough to feed the family for a year. When June comes, my stocks are depleted and I struggle to buy food at the market and pay the bills." Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
31 Oct 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: Nineteen-year-old Djiee has been helping her father since she finished school last year. In Senegal, a large part of the population relies on traditional family farming for their livelihoods and are very vulnerable to weather conditions. Today, half a million people need urgent food assistance but forecasts expect this number to double as the lean season approaches. Credit: OCHA/Berenice Van Den Driessche.
31 Oct 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: “Rains came a month late this year. The millet and groundnuts did not grow well, but I am hopeful that the niebe will be good." Beyhrar and his family received 4kg of high quality seeds from FAO. Timely mitigation measures can prevent poor households from sliding into crisis as the lean season comes. Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
1 Nov 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: School attendance is a concern for poor rural communities. “Education is free but when poverty hits the family, parents sometimes take a daughter out of school to help at home. A pupil from Ndeye’s class dropped out earlier this year and I just saw her selling peanuts at the road junction,” says the Director of the village’s school. Credit: OCHA/Ivo Brandau
1 Nov 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: Beyhrar Gueye makes a point that all his children complete secondary school. “School teaches you everything you need to make it through life,” he says. Credit: OCHA/Berenice Van Den Driessche.
1 Nov 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal: Khoudia, Ndeye's mother, prepares sandwiches with freshly picked niebe spread that she sells daily at the village school. “I have to sell part of our production to pay the utility bills, rice or medication for my children.” Credit: OCHA/Berenice Van Den Driessche.
1 November 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal. The three sisters, Fally, Djiee and Ndeye return from a day harvesting. Assistance by the Senegalese government and FAO during the cultivation season partially mitigated the the impact of the poor rain season in Kelle Gueye. The government and WFP also support families with food distributions during the lean season. Credit: OCHA/Berenice Van Den Driessche
1 Nov 2014, Kelle Gueye, Senegal. In 2014, 3 million people were in need of humanitarian assistance across Senegal. The scale of the needs across the country for 2015 and the planned response of the humanitarian community will be announced in February 2015. Credit: OCHA/Berenice Van Den Driessche.
