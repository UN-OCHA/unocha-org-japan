Over the past two months, some 250 children have been prevented from being trafficked and unnecessarily or illegally placed in children’s care homes, according to UNICEF. Part of the credit for this success goes to child protection officers at checkpoints like this one, in Bandeu, Sindhupalchowk, where 28 children have been rescued. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Dil Maya Rumba is one of the Government's District Child Welfare Board (DCWB) inspectors. “If we find children being moved out of the district, we inquire with their guardians to find answers on where and why they are being moved. We let them go only after seeing the authorization from the DCWB and other governmental entities,” she explains. If no authorization is presented, the children are reunited with their families. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
As a public bus arrives at the checkpoint, the inspectors and a police constable immediately approach the bus and look for potential victims of child trafficking onboard. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Sometimes, the inspectors and the police have to jostle for space with street vendors, who compete to sell candy, water and fruits to the passengers on the bus. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Once inside, the inspectors ask questions to the children and the adults accompanying them. According to inspector Pramod Aryal (not seen in this image), lately, there has been a rise in cases of child trafficking. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Inside the bus, the inspectors also distribute pamphlets on preventing child trafficking to passengers, who sometimes frown upon them for “the hassles.” Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Every public bus passing through the checkpoint has to register with the child protection inspectors. Since 24 May, nearly 4,000 buses have been registered at this checkpoint. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Sometimes police have to intervene when the bus passengers refuse to cooperate with the inspectors. “Buses coming from Barhabise don’t stop easily,” said inspector Kiran Giri (right). “The police helps us stop them so we can carry out our work.” Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
When the inspectors and the police feel the need for deeper questioning, the child and his/her accompanying adult are taken out of the bus. On 30 June, for example, 11 children travelling "under suspicious circumstances” were rescued and reunited with their families. Photo: OCHA/Tilak Pokharel.
Keeping children safe and in school is key in preventing child trafficking. In the Sindhupalchowk district, education groups have established more than 500 temporary learning spaces. Photo: Save the Children/Bijay Gajmer.
