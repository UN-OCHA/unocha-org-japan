Daily Brief: Yemen, Eritrea, El Nino/Ethiopia, DR Congo

28 Jan 2016

ASG/DERC Kang meets school children lining up in front of a dental school bus.
Credit: OCHA/Laila Bourhil
An update on Yemen; ASG Kang's visit to Eritrea and Ethiopia; Humanitarian Coordinator in DRC expresses serious concerns over the material and protection needs of the civilian population.

Yemen

  • The Humanitarian Coordinator for Yemen, Jamie McGoldrick, has been in Al Hudaydah Governorate for the past three days, meeting affected people, including those displaced from Hajjah, and visiting projects where humanitarian partners are providing water, sanitation and food.

Eritrea

  • Assistant Secretary-General for Humanitarian Affairs and Deputy Emergency Relief Coordinator, Kyung-wha Kang, wrapped up a three-day visit to Eritrea yesterday, urging greater support for vulnerable communities and reiterating the readiness of United Nations development and humanitarian actors to work together in support of Government endeavours to meet basic needs and build the resilience of the most vulnerable communities in the country.
  • ASG Kang met with the Minister for Foreign Affairs; the Head of Political Affairs of the People’s Front for Democracy and Justice and Presidential Advisor, the Minister for Health and other senior Government officials as well as humanitarian, development and donor representatives.
  • Ms. Kang visited Government-led nutrition and basic health programmes in the Maekel region, that help thousands of children and mothers with the support of UN agencies. She said, “Programmes that provide supplementary feeding, immunization and nutrition surveillance are vital to reducing malnutrition and building resilience. Eritrea has made significant progress in improving child and maternal health, and I urge partners to continue supporting national and international efforts.”

Read full press release.

El Niño/Ethiopia

  • Deputy Emergency Relief Coordinator and ASG for Humanitarian Affairs Kyung-wha Kang briefed media in Addis today on the humanitarian impact of El Niño, with the African Union Commissioner for Political Affairs, Aisha Laraba Abdullahi, in the margins of the AU Summit.
  • “What is new about this El Niño is its strength. By some estimates, it is as strong as what was known as the super El Niño of 1997/1998, which killed over 23,000 people and displaced over a million people by some estimates. The damage alone was estimated to be about US $ 45 billion and the cost on global economic output was huge. This at the end of the day could be equal in terms of its destructive power," warned ASG Kang. “Just to give you an idea of how impactful this has been just in Ethiopia: at the beginning of 2015, we were talking about some three million people in need of food assistance. Now we are talking about 10.2 million people in need of food assistance. And this number could grow if we don’t take urgent measures to restore livelihoods in agriculture sectors, for example by distributing seeds so that they can be ready to plant when the rainy seasons come.”
  • She also said that, “The human consequences of the El Niño effect - on drought and drought-like conditions - in many countries of this region, South Africa, Latin America, and the Pacific Islands, have been huge. The humanitarian community has been tracking the situation very carefully and our main call at this point is we need more resources. Donors have stepped up but we need more.”

Read more about the humanitarian impact of El Niño.

DR Congo

  • The Humanitarian Coordinator in DRC, Dr Mamadou Diallo, wrapped up a two-day visit to the northeastern Ituri Province.
  • He visited Geti, Bukiringi and Komanda, which have seengross human rights violations over the past two years by parties to the conflict, resulting in the displacement of close to 200,000 people.
  • “Speaking to displaced people and humanitarian actors on the ground, I have heard stories of unspeakable suffering and human rights violations inflicted on civilians,” said HC Diallo, adding that he was deeply concerned by the material and protection needs of the civilian population, and the gaps that remain, despite a concerted response.
  • The Humanitarian Coordinator insisted that protection of civilians must be the primary goal for the Congolese civilian and military authorities, for the United Nations forces and the humanitarian family, and urged donors to provide sustained and especially flexible funding to humanitarian actors, enabling them to deliver as quickly and as effectively as possible.
